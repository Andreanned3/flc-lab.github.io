Introduction

We are a multidisciplinary group using super-resolution microscopy in combination with machine learning to investigate the structure and function of neurobiological systems.

Read More

Super-Resolution

Microscopy has revolutionized the biological sciences, but there are many biological questions that conventional microscopy is unable to answer due to the diffraction of light in conventional systems. A conventional light microscope is unable to visualize objects smaller than the diffraction limit of ~250 nm. Our lab uses STED super-resolution microscopy to visualize objects smaller than the diffraction limit.

Machine Learning

We apply machine learning techniques to optimize image acquisition and analysis. Our machine learning algorithms can automate tasks such as segmentation and quantification that would otherwise take hundreds of hours to accomplish manually. As a microscopy lab, we produce large quantities of images, and machine learning methods require lots of images to train: there is a natural interest in training machine learning algorithms on our acquired data. We also develop machine learning methods to optimize the acquisition parameters and extract as much information as possible from our images.

Quantitive Analysis

We use optical nanoscopy to explore several aspects of neuronal structure and function. Our areas of interest include synaptic protein localization, mitochondrial dynamics, and functional imaging.
